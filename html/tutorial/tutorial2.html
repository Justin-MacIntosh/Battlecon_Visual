Turn Order

A given Beat (turn) of Battlecon begins with CHOOSING ATTACK PAIRS. Each player deciding which pair they will play privately, and playing them face down. Then comes the ANTE PHASE. Some characters in Battlecon have tokens they can use to ante during this phase. If you're familiar with games like poker, anteing is not a new concept. It works much the same here if both characters have tokens to ante. If you are unfamiliar with how anteing works as a concept, click HERE. These anted tokens can do a wide variey of things (characters in Battlecon work in unique ways, too many to list here).

After both players have their anteing done, both players REVEAL their attack. Next, CLASHes are resolved. A Clash occurs when two pairs have the same priority. In this case, both players secretly pick a new Base for their Pair. The Style card remains intact, and when both players agree on a new Base card, they reveal them and use it to replace their old Base. For the rest of the beat, the players will play with their new attack pairs (The originally selected Style, and the newly selected Base).

This is where the text on the cards starts to matter. Both cards will likely have effects. This is when Reveal effects take place. Any ability on a player's attck pair with the text "Reveal:" before it happens now. If both players have effcts that trigger at the same given time, the effects trigger in Priority order. The pair that has the highest total priority value triggers first.
